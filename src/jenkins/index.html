Today we're announcing Jenkins instances for projects and/or teams! Here are some of our terms/information:1. We own the servers where Jenkins is running. 2. We keep the Jenkins version updated, you keep the plugins updated. 3. You get to become admin for the Jenkins instance and you decide who gets access and what plugins to install. 4. To help with configuration and troubleshooting, we in the SDPTeam will also be admins on the instances. 5. When we upgrade Jenkins, we'll have an announcement about which instances will be affected. 6. If some plugins stops working after upgrade, it's up to you to update/fix/replace that plugin. You can of course ask us for help. 7. Instances will be available under ci.statoil.no/ 8. Servers will be backed up with virtual machine snapshots at first, but later we'll have file-level backup in place as well. 9. Servers will be placed in AWS unless you ask for something else. 10. Instances run in docker, meaning they are very good at running Jenkins, nothing else. In other words, you should add build slaves if you want things done. Unable to find any good way to serve Jenkins ssh-pub-key automatically (used when setting up slaves), you'll get this key when your instance is ready. After this, you can always just ask us for it.What do we need from you to create a Jenkins instance? 1. Your uri-prefix, typically your team/project name (see #7 above) 2. Statoil shortname for one of the admins. This admin will decide further who gets access. 3.
